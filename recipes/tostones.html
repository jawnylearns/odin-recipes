Tostones

Description

Tostones are a prominent dish found within the Caribbean and Latin America. They are crisp, flattened plantains that are usually served as appetizers or side dishes.

Ingredients

For around 3-4 servings

  • 2 green(unripe) plantains (about 6 ounces each)
  • Neutral oil, such as vegetable or canola oil, for frying
  • Kosher salt, to taste

Preperation

Step 1.

  1. Trim and discard the ends of the plantains.
  2. Use a praing knife to cut a slit along the length of the plantain to easily remove the peel, carefully avoiding cutting into the flesh.
  3. If the peel is too firm, use a spoon to lift the edges, then discard the peel.
  4. Slice the plantain crosswise into 1-inch rounds (about 8 pieces per plantain).

Step 2.

  1. In a large skillet, heat about 1/8-inch vegetable oil over medium.
  2. When the oil shimmers, add the plantains, flat-side down.
  3. Fry until beginning to turn golden, about 1 minute per side.

Step 3.

  1. While keeping the heat on, transfer plantains to a cutting board.
  2. Working with one piece at a time, use a tostonera or large flat-bottomed cup to gently press each plantains to about 1/2-inch thick. (If using a cup, keep it as a centered as possible to flatten the plantains evenly.)

Step 4.

  1. Working in batches if necessary to avoid crowding the pan, pan-fry the flattened plantains until golden-brown, 1 to 2 minutes per side.

Step 5.

  1. Using a slotted spoon, transfer tostones to a paper towel -lined plate to drain.
  2. While hot, season with salt to taste.
  3. Time to Serve! :)
